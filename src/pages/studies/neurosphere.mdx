[neurosphere.io →](https://neurosphere.io)

Neurosphere is a Berlin-based startup building mindfulness products powered by real-time EEG neurofeedback. A textile headband reads brainwave activity and turns that signal into audio feedback through a companion app.

I joined to improve the product experience and help shape a broader ecosystem around neurofeedback. My work grew from UX on the existing product into product direction across mobile, web, brand, and physical touchpoints.

## Mobile app & neurofeedback flows

Mobile app UX and the core neurofeedback feedback flows: meditation selection, live sessions with device connection, progress tracking, and session summaries that translate EEG data into something users can actually understand.

## Web platform & website

Built out the marketing website including payment flows, and contributed to the web platform that supported the product and course experience.

## Brand direction

Shaped the visual identity, tone of voice, and how the product presented itself across every touchpoint, from digital to print.

## Hardware experiments

Early work around how EEG signals could translate into meaningful user feedback. A lot of hands-on iteration with the hardware team, testing what works when the signal is clean, what happens when it's noisy, and how to make the experience feel clear and trustworthy rather than gimmicky.

The project taught me a lot about designing under real technical constraints. It was a small, fast-moving startup environment with a lot of experimentation across product, brand, and hardware.
